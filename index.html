Every Monday morning, one economics student records what the same fixed basket costs at four Folsom grocery stores — Walmart, Target, Safeway, and Bel Air — item by item, straight from each retailer's own published store prices. National CPI tells you what food costs on average, months later. This tells you what it costs here, this week — measured, not estimated.

01 — The number

Grocery inflation, measured on foot

The index is a weighted average of price changes across five food categories, benchmarked to the first week of collection (= 100). Weights follow the BLS food-at-home relative-importance structure, so a move in this line means the same thing a CPI move does — just for Folsom, and weekly instead of bimonthly.

04 — The method

How it's measured

1

Fixed basket

The same named items, in the same package sizes, at the same stores — every single week. No substitutions; if an item is out of stock, it's flagged, not swapped.

2

Collected consistently

Prices recorded every Monday morning from each retailer's own website or app, with the exact Folsom store selected. Regular listed prices only — promotions and member-only prices are logged separately, never blended in.

3

Weighted like the CPI

Category price relatives are combined with BLS-style food-at-home weights (a Laspeyres index), so the composite behaves like a true price index, not a raw price average.

4

Published open

Every observation lives in a public CSV on GitHub. Anyone can audit the math, rebuild the index, or extend it to their own city.

This is an independent student research project. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or informed by any retailer's internal data — every price is the retailer's own publicly listed price for its named Folsom store. Store names can be anonymized on request.
