# GET IT NOTICED — The Cart Index launch playbook

Golden rule for every post, email, and pitch: **lead with the finding, not the project.** "The same 14 groceries cost $13 more at Bel Air than Walmart in Folsom this week" gets attention. "I made a website" does not. The project rides along behind the number.

---

## Phase 0 — Before launch (this week)

- [ ] Deploy the site (README setup, ~20 min) and buy the custom domain (~$12/yr, e.g. folsomcartindex.com). The domain is what makes this feel like an institution instead of a school project.
- [ ] Add free analytics so impact is measurable from day one: GoatCounter or Plausible (one script tag) — or at minimum watch GitHub's built-in traffic stats.
- [ ] Clear it with work: read the Raley's employee handbook section on outside activities/media, or ask a manager. Keep the written answer.
- [ ] Do collection #1 on Monday morning; replace the sample CSV. **Do not announce until at least 2–3 weeks of real data exist** — a chart with one dot isn't a story. Collect quietly for 2–3 Mondays first.
- [ ] Create an `IMPACT.md` in the repo and log everything from here on (visitors, mentions, weeks, observations).

## Phase 1 — Launch (weeks 3–4 of data)

- [ ] **LinkedIn launch post.** Structure: the hook number → one screenshot of the dashboard → 3 sentences on method ("same 14 items, 4 Folsom stores, every Monday, weighted like the CPI, all data public") → the link. Add "Founder, The Cart Index" to your Experience section the same day.
- [ ] **Folsom Lake College.** Email the economics faculty and the professor whose statistical economics course you'll assist: 4 sentences + link, and ask one specific question ("is there a methodological weakness you'd poke at?"). Professors respond to being asked for critique, and one becoming a champion is worth more than any press hit. Ask if the college's communications office features student projects.
- [ ] **Local community channels.** Nextdoor (Folsom), r/Sacramento, and Facebook Folsom community groups — value-first framing: "I price the same 14 grocery items at 4 Folsom stores every Monday. Here's which cart was cheapest this month and by how much." Answer every comment; the questions people ask become next week's content.
- [ ] **Bridge2Transfer network.** Your chapter is a built-in distribution channel — and the future recruiting pipeline for a second city.

## Phase 2 — Institutions & media (weeks 4–10)

- [ ] **City of Folsom.** You've already participated in the city's "Ideas for Change" civic panel — use that thread. Email the Communications & Community Engagement office and the Economic Development department: offer the index as a free public resource for residents, and ask whether it could be mentioned in the city newsletter or resident communications. Template below.
- [ ] **Folsom Chamber of Commerce** — same offer; chambers love hyperlocal data and member newsletters need content.
- [ ] **Local press, in escalating order:** Folsom Telegraph → Folsom Times → Sacramento Bee (business/consumer desk) → KCRA 3 / ABC10 consumer reporters. Pitch template below. The evergreen local-TV angle: "19-year-old grocery clerk builds Folsom's only weekly food inflation tracker — and found families can save $X00/year."
- [ ] **Folsom Lake College newspaper/newsletter** and any student media.

## Phase 3 — The recurring engine (every Monday, forever)

- [ ] Post the Monday number on LinkedIn — same format weekly, so it becomes a series people expect. Consistency here is the single highest-ROI action on this list.
- [ ] Start "The Monday Cart" — a free one-paragraph email (Buttondown or Substack, both free). Subscriber count becomes a headline impact metric.
- [ ] When the BLS releases Sacramento-area CPI (every other month): publish "Folsom vs. the official number" the same day. This is your most press-worthy recurring moment — you have weekly local data; they have bimonthly metro data.
- [ ] When any single item moves sharply (eggs, coffee): a focused mini-post. Shrinkflation catches (package size changes) are their own story.

## Phase 4 — Milestones & scale

- [ ] **Week 10+:** post the chart to r/dataisbeautiful and Show HN on Hacker News ("Show HN: I price the same grocery basket at 4 stores in my city every week"). Open-data + hyperlocal + student-run does well there; traffic spikes go straight into IMPACT.md.
- [ ] **Week 12 / 26 / 52:** recap posts with cumulative stats (observations, measured savings, CPI divergence). The week-52 recap is your application-season centerpiece.
- [ ] **Second city.** Recruit one student (Bridge2Transfer contact, or a commenter) to fork the repo for their city. The moment a second city publishes, the story upgrades from "my project" to "a student-run price-index network I founded."
- [ ] **The paper.** Semester-end write-up — *Weekly Hyperlocal Grocery Price Dynamics in Folsom, CA* — reviewed by the professor you'll IA for, posted to the repo as a PDF.

## Templates

### City of Folsom email

> Subject: A free weekly grocery price index for Folsom residents
>
> Hi [name] — I'm Dustin, a Folsom resident, economics student at Folsom Lake College, and a participant in the city's Ideas for Change panel earlier this year. Since [month], I've been publishing The Cart Index (folsomcartindex.com): every Monday I record what the same 14-item grocery basket costs at Walmart, Target, Safeway, and Bel Air here in Folsom, from each retailer's published prices, and compute a weekly local food-inflation index — something that doesn't exist anywhere else, since federal CPI covers our metro only every other month. This week's data shows a family can save about $[X]/week by choosing the cheapest cart. It's free, independent, and all data is public. Would this be a fit for the city newsletter or resident resources page? Happy to share more or present it briefly.

### Press pitch (Telegraph / Bee / TV)

> Subject: Folsom teen tracks grocery prices at 4 local stores every week — the gap is $[X] a cart
>
> Hi [name] — I'm 19, I bag groceries part-time in Folsom, and I study economics at Folsom Lake College. Every Monday since [month] I've priced the identical 14-item basket at Walmart, Target, Safeway, and Bel Air in Folsom and published the results as a weekly local inflation index — the only one of its kind, since official CPI for Sacramento comes out every other month. This week the same cart ranged from $[low] to $[high] — about $[X × 52]/year for a typical family. Everything is public at folsomcartindex.com. Happy to walk you through the data or the [n] weeks of trends. Best, Dustin [phone]

> Cheapest full cart: [store], $[total] — $[gap] less than the priciest.
> Mover of the week: [item] [±x]%.

## What NOT to do

- Don't announce with fewer than 3 weeks of data.
- Don't frame anything as "store X is ripping you off" — you compare prices, you don't editorialize against retailers (one of them signs your paychecks). "Premium service tier" vs "discount tier" is the vocabulary.
- Don't break the Monday streak. If a week must be missed, publish the gap honestly.
- Don't inflate numbers anywhere. The whole asset is credibility.
